How to Setup Google Sheets OAuth2 client

  1. Go to the Google Cloud Console.
  2. Click on "Select a project" and then on "New Project".
  3. Name your project, then click on "Create".
  4. On the project dashboard, click on "Enable APIs and Services".
  5. Search for "Google Sheets API" and enable it.
  6. Go back to the dashboard, click on "Credentials", then "Create Credentials" and choose "OAuth client ID".
  7. Configure the consent screen as required, then choose "Web application" for the Application Type. Enter your extension's ID in the "Authorized JavaScript origins" field and save.
  8. Copy the generated client ID for use in the extension.

How to Find Google Sheets ID and Define Range

Your Google Sheets ID is part of the URL when you're viewing your spreadsheet. For example, if your Google Sheets URL is "https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1a2b3c4d5e6f7g8/edit", then your Google Sheets ID is "1a2b3c4d5e6f7g8".

If you have populated Column A of your Google Sheet with the quotes or text you want to be pasted, then you would enter the range as "A:A". Here are some examples of other ranges:

  • "A1:A100" if your quotes are in cells A1 to A100.
  • "A1:B100" if your quotes are in cells from A1 to B100.
  • "Sheet2!A:A" if your quotes are in Column A of Sheet2.

Entering OAuth2 client details in the extension

  1. Click on the Quote Injector Extension icon in your browser.
  2. Click on the link to the extension options page.
  3. Enter the client ID you just generated into the Client ID field.
  4. Enter your Google Sheets ID and the range of cells where your quotes are located into the corresponding fields, then click save.

Using the Extension

  1. When composing a new email in Gmail, a random quote from your Google Sheets document will be inserted at the bottom of the email composer.
  2. If you want to refresh the pool of quotes, click on the extension icon and press the "Refresh Quotes" button in the popup.
  3. If you need to change your settings, go to the extension options page by following the link in the popup.
